Section A

Understanding the Author's Idea

Question 1

What is the main argument presented in the poster?

Hint: The main argument is the central idea or opinion that the author wants readers to believe.

Question 2

Identify the thesis statement of the analytical text. Why do you think this sentence is the thesis?

Hint: A thesis statement is the main claim or opinion of the author, usually found in the introduction.

Question 3

How does the author support their argument in the text? Mention at least two supporting ideas.

Hint: Supporting ideas are reasons, facts, or examples that help explain or prove the author's opinion.

Question 4

After listening to the audio explanation, do you think it helps clarify the author's message? Why?

Hint: Clarify means to make something easier to understand.

Analyzing the Visual Elements

Question 5

Hint: Think about how the picture helps explain or strengthen the message in the text.

Question 6

Hint: Visual elements include pictures, colors, icons, or design used in the poster.

Question 7

Hint: Think about whether the text alone is enough to understand the message.

Critical Thinking

Question 8

Hint: Convincing means able to make people believe or agree with something.

Question 9

Question 10

Hint: You can express a different opinion from the author.
